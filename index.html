📖 Project Description
The alluvial valley of the Mississippi River is an extensive area harboring hundreds of lakes created by fluvial dynamics. These floodplain lakes are scattered throughout the valley and carved over thousands of years by shifting river courses and other hydro-fluvial processes associated with contemporary and prehistoric rivers. These lakes have significant ecological importance as they support a large component of North American biodiversity. We used remote sensing to catalog lakes, to characterize morphology, and to characterize connectivity. We identified over 1,300 permanent lakes totaling over 100,000 ha. We also list the likelihood for bigheaded carp inhabiting the lake. Details are available in the listed publications.
